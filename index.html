The Beatles

Irene Moore

The Beatles, a British boy band formed in 1960, were comprised of four members

  1. Paul McCartney
  2. John Lennon
  3. Ringo Starr
  4. George Harrison

Paul McCartney, John Lennon, and George Harrison were the orignal members of the band. Ringo Starr replaced the band's orignal drummer, Pete Best, in 1962. The Beatles caught fame with their first single, "Love Me Do," released in 1962. Over the course of their career, they released over 200 songs. However, the band broke up in 1970 after almost a decade.
